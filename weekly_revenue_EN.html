The three strategies
Raise the price
21-27 June · 19-25 July · 2-8 August. Weeks that are full or nearly full (high occupancy, above 2025) but with ADR down versus a year ago. They fill up regardless: you are leaving margin on the table. Push rates up on the remaining rooms.
Signal: occupancy ≥ last year + price below last year
Stay the course
28 Jun-4 Jul · 5-11 July · 9-15 August. Working well as is: rising price and solid incoming demand. On the mid-August peak (9-15 Aug) bookings come in at €261 — push the price and apply minimum-stay, but don't change the approach.
Signal: rising price + healthy pace
Generate demand
26 Jul-1 August. The emptiest week (20%) with a slow pace. Work it on volume — packages, promotions, direct-channel push — before the date approaches.
Signal: low occupancy + slow pace
Watch closely
16-22 August. The only week below 2025 (-10 points), with a very slow pace — but incoming bookings pay €283, the highest rate of all. The high price is holding back volume: decide whether the post-peak tail is worth more at full price with few rooms, or with a targeted offer that unlocks occupancy.
Signal: occupancy below last year + very high price + slow pace
